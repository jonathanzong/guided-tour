STUDIOLAB

Student Artwork

Welcome to the Studiolab gallery. Here we have on display work by students that happened in the Studiolab or relates to the mission of the lab.

The medium for these works includes everything from physical construction materials, electronics, and algorithms.

Drew Wallace ‘17

The Leap Motion Controller is a hand tracking device designed to let humans "interact with digital content... just as you would in the real world".

A Show of Hands is a series of vignettes that illuminate and explore the Leap's failings in this goal. The physical-virtual divide needs not be overcome, but understood and embraced. The hands' mock-physical touches are playful and joyous - not in spite of their conspicuous unreality, but because of it.

Helen Lin, Kathleen Ma, Mariah Wilson ‘18

Mother Womb is a full-sensory experience while evoking imagined memories and escaping reality. A portable tent envelops the viewer with visual, auditory and tactile sensations.

"This was a literal transformation of synthetic materials used for the 'womb' to create a natural environment," said Lin. "As a visual artist, I don't normally work with physical things. So I learned a lot by working on this physical environment, figuring out what works and what doesn't."

Jonathan Zong ‘18

∆: light sound motion

A wave is energy that travels through space, oscillates over time.

Sound and light are characterized by the frequency and amplitude of their constituent waves.

Pitch is analogous to color; they are both described by frequency.
Volume is analogous to intensity; they are both described by amplitude.
Δ is a light harmonograph that examines the dimensionality of these wave features.

In particular, how light and sound act in motion through space and time — how they are perceived in the interstices of change along these dimensions.
